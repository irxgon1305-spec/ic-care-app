What is IC?

The gentle, plain-language version.

Interstitial cystitis (IC) is a chronic bladder condition — not an infection — where the bladder wall becomes irritated and inflamed. It causes pelvic pain, a frequent urge to pee, and sometimes burning. You might also hear it called "painful bladder syndrome."

The exact cause isn't fully understood, but it involves a sensitive or compromised bladder lining that lets irritants reach the bladder wall. It's very real, sometimes frustrating — but very manageable with the right habits.

The kind reminder 🌿

IC looks different for everyone. Some days are calm, some days are loud, and both are completely normal. Living well with IC is mostly about noticing your own patterns and being gentle with yourself.

📓 Tiny habit that helps: jot down what you eat and how you feel a few hours later. Over time you'll build your own personal map of what your bladder loves and what it doesn't.
🌊

Why Flares Happen

The flare cycle, without the jargon.

A flare is when symptoms spike for a few hours or days. Flares are usually trigger-based — something sets off an underlying sensitivity that's already there.

The most common triggers 🌊

  • Certain foods & drinks (coffee, alcohol, citrus, tomatoes, spicy food)
  • Hormonal shifts around your period
  • Stress and poor sleep
  • Holding urine too long

Think asthma or migraines 🔁

There's an underlying sensitivity, and specific things set it off. The good news: once you know your triggers, flares become more predictable — and easier to soften before they get loud.

💡 Triggers are common, not universal. Something that bothers one person may be totally fine for you. Test slowly, one thing at a time, and trust what your body reports back.
🌙

Around Your Period

Why some days feel extra sensitive.

Flaring right before or during your period is extremely common — it's not in your head. There are two main reasons behind it.

1. Hormonal shifts 🌙

The drop in estrogen before your period makes the bladder and pelvic nerves more sensitive — so everyday signals can feel bigger during this window.

2. Prostaglandins 🌸

The inflammatory compounds released during menstruation also irritate the bladder and surrounding tissue, adding to the tenderness.

You can prepare for it 🤍

Because this window is predictable, you can get ahead of it:

  • Go extra gentle on trigger foods 5–7 days before your period
  • Lean on heat — a heating pad on your lower belly is lovely
  • Rest more; permission granted
🗓️ Try noting where you are in your cycle in your food diary. Spotting the pattern makes the tender days feel less surprising — and easier to plan around.

What's Happening in Your Body

Naming the feelings, so they feel less scary.

The urge with little output 💧

Your inflamed bladder sends "full" signals to your brain even when there's barely anything there. So you feel the urge, go, and little comes out. The bladder is essentially over-firing its signals — it's not you being dramatic.

The burning drips 🔥

When urine is concentrated, it's more acidic. That acidic urine passing through already-sensitive tissue burns. It can feel exactly like an infection even when it isn't.

Why drinking more can hurt 🫧

Forcing large amounts of water to "flush" fills the bladder faster and adds pressure to inflamed walls. Small, steady sips work better than gulping.

🚑 Gentle reminder: if a symptom is suddenly new or much worse — fever, chills, visible blood, or severe one-sided pain — that's worth a same-day call to a doctor to rule out infection.

Gentle Daily Habits

Small, kind things that can make each day a little easier.

You don't have to do all of these — even one on a hard day counts. Think of it as a menu of comforts, not a checklist. 💗

💊 Take Prelief before meals

It neutralizes most of the food acid before it reaches your bladder, giving you a little more freedom at the table.

💧 Sip water steadily all day

Steady sips keep your pee less concentrated and gentler on the bladder. Don't gulp large amounts at once — slow and steady is kinder.

🍽️ Eat smaller meals

A very full stomach adds pressure around the bladder. Smaller, more frequent meals tend to sit easier.

🔥 Use heat

A heating pad on your lower belly is one of the best comfort tools there is — simple, cozy, and surprisingly effective.

🧘 Rest and de-stress

Stress and poor sleep both lower your bladder's threshold, so calm and good rest genuinely help. Slowing down is part of feeling well.

📓 Track what you eat & how you feel

Your personal pattern matters most. A quick daily jot builds your own trigger map over time — no perfection needed, just gentle curiosity.

🌙 Go extra gentle before your period

The week before your period is a predictable tender window — ease off trigger foods and lean on your comforts ahead of time.

🤍 On a flare day, the only habit that matters is being soft with yourself. Water, warmth, rest, and a safe snack — that's more than enough.
